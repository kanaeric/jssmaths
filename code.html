Place Value of Decimals

1. The Photo Finish! (Problem to Solve) 🏁

PROBLEM SITUATION 😳

Nigeria's hero, Tobi Amusan, runs a world-record race! Her time is announced as 12.12 seconds. The '1' and '2' digits appear twice!

But does the first '1' mean the same thing as the second '1'? Does the first '2' have the same value as the second '2'? What makes them different? 🤔

The secret is the decimal point! It's a tiny dot with a huge job. It separates the whole numbers from the tiny, fractional parts. Today, we will explore the amazing world to the right of the decimal point and master decimal place value! 🚀

2. Decimals in Your Daily Nigerian Life 🇳🇬

Once you understand decimal places, you'll see them everywhere, helping you be more precise.

Money (Naira and Kobo) 💰

When you see a price like ₦150.75, the '.75' part is a decimal. The 7 is in the tenths place (70 kobo) and the 5 is in the hundredths place (5 kobo).

Fuel Prices at the Station ⛽

The price of petrol might be ₦617.50 per litre. That '.5' is in the tenths place, representing half a Naira.

Tailoring and Measurements 📏

A tailor in Aba might measure a piece of Ankara fabric as 3.5 metres. The '.5' tells them it's exactly halfway to the next metre, allowing for a perfect cut.

Athletics and Sports Timing 🏃‍♀️

In sports, every fraction of a second counts! A time of 10.45 seconds is faster than 10.5 seconds because the '4' in the tenths place is smaller than the '5'.

Scientific Measurements 🔬

In your science class, you might weigh a small amount of a chemical, like 4.25 grams. The '.25' gives you the precise measurement needed for your experiment to work!

3. Your Decimal Dictionary 📖

Mastering these words is the key to understanding the topic! 🔑

Decimal Point

The dot (.) that separates the whole number part (on the left) from the fractional part (on the right).

Tenths

The first place to the right of the decimal point. Its value is the digit divided by 10 (e.g., 0.7 = ⁷/₁₀).

Hundredths

The second place to the right of the decimal point. Its value is the digit divided by 100 (e.g., 0.03 = ³/₁₀₀).

Thousandths

The third place to the right of the decimal point. Its value is the digit divided by 1000 (e.g., 0.009 = ⁹/₁₀₀₀).

4. The Place Value Chart: The Full Map 🗺️

The decimal point acts like a mirror. The place values on the right side are a reflection of the ones on the left, but they are fractions and end with "-ths".

Hundreds Tens Ones . Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
100 10 1 . ¹/₁₀ ¹/₁₀₀ ¹/₁₀₀₀

Let's find the place value of each digit in the number 27.645.

1

Analyse the Whole Number Part

Look at the digits to the left of the decimal point.

  • The 2 is in the Tens place. Its value is 20.
  • The 7 is in the Ones place. Its value is 7.
2

Analyse the Decimal Part

Look at the digits to the right of the decimal point.

  • The 6 is in the Tenths place. Its value is 0.6 or ⁶/₁₀.
  • The 4 is in the Hundredths place. Its value is 0.04 or ⁴/₁₀₀.
  • The 5 is in the Thousandths place. Its value is 0.005 or ⁵/₁₀₀₀.

5. Case Study: The Tailor's Precision 📏

Mr. Ade, a tailor in Lagos, is measuring fabric for a very special agbada. He measures a piece to be 4.375 metres long. He needs to understand the value of each digit to ensure a perfect fit.

What is the place value of the digit '7' in his measurement?

Let's analyse the measurement 4.375:

1. Locate the decimal point.

2. Look at the digits to the right:

  • The '3' is in the first position, the Tenths place.
  • The '7' is in the second position, the Hundredths place.
  • The '5' is in the third position, the Thousandths place.

✅ Final Answer: The place of the digit '7' is the Hundredths place. Its value is 0.07 metres.

6. Financial Focus: Understanding Kobo 💰

Chiamaka looks at a receipt from a supermarket. The total is ₦2,548.75.

Her teacher asks her: "What is the value of the '7' and what is the value of the '5' after the decimal point, in terms of Kobo?"

Let's break down the Kobo:

Remember, there are 100 Kobo in 1 Naira. This means the Hundredths place is the Kobo place.

• The '7' is in the Tenths place. A tenth of a Naira is 10 Kobo. So, the value is 7 × 10 Kobo = 70 Kobo.

• The '5' is in the Hundredths place. A hundredth of a Naira is 1 Kobo. So, the value is 5 × 1 Kobo = 5 Kobo.

Together, they make up the 75 Kobo in the price.

7. Maths Magic: The "-ths" Suffix Rule! ✨

The Easiest Way to Name the Places

It's easy to get confused between "tens" and "tenths" or "hundreds" and "hundredths".

Just remember this simple rule:

If the place is to the RIGHT of the dot, its name ends in "-ths" and it's a tiny piece!

  • Tens → Whole number (big)
  • Tenths → Fraction (small)
  • Hundreds → Whole number (very big)
  • Hundredths → Fraction (very small)

8. Watch Out! Decimal Traps! ⚠️

Mistake: Forgetting the Decimal Point is the Center

Students sometimes start counting places from the far right of the number, ignoring the decimal point.

❌ Wrong Way

In 12.34, saying the '3' is in the Tens place and the '2' is in the Hundreds place. This is wrong!

✅ Right Way

The decimal point is the center. To the left is Ones (2), then Tens (1). To the right is Tenths (3), then Hundredths (4).

Mistake: The Imaginary "Oneths" Place

There is no "oneths" place! The places to the right of the dot start immediately with Tenths.

❌ Wrong Way

In 0.5, thinking the 5 is in the "oneths" place.

✅ Right Way

The first place to the right of the decimal is ALWAYS the Tenths place. The 5 in 0.5 is in the Tenths place.

9. Practice & Challenge Zone 🧠

Standard Practice Problem

In the number 58.194, what is the place value of the digit 9?

Hint: The digit 9 is the second digit to the right of the decimal point. What is the name of that place?

1. Locate the digit: The 9 is in the second position after the decimal point.

2. Name the place: The first place is Tenths, so the second place is Hundredths.

✅ Final Answer: The place value of the 9 is Hundredths (or its value is 0.09).

⭐ Challenge Problem ⭐

In the number 61.283, what is the product of the face value of the digit in the Tenths place and the face value of the digit in the Thousandths place?

Hint: "Product" means multiply. First, find the digit in the tenths place. Then find the digit in the thousandths place. Finally, multiply their face values (the digits themselves).

1. Digit in the Tenths place: The first digit after the dot is 2. Its face value is 2.

2. Digit in the Thousandths place: The third digit after the dot is 3. Its face value is 3.

3. Find the Product:

2 × 3 = 6

✅ Final Answer: The product is 6.

10. Your Golden Rules for Decimal Place Value 📜

Remember these simple rules to master decimal places!

  1. THE DOT IS KING: The decimal point is the center of the number world. It separates wholes on the left from parts on the right. 👑
  2. WHOLE ON THE LEFT: To the left of the dot, the places are Ones, Tens, Hundreds, etc. (big values). ⬅️
  3. PARTS ON THE RIGHT: To the right of the dot, the places are Tenths, Hundredths, Thousandths, etc. (tiny values). ➡️
  4. REMEMBER THE "-THS": Every place value name to the right of the dot ends in "-ths". 🗣️
