Decimal Addition!

Addition of Decimals

1. The Hook & Problem Situation 🤔

PROBLEM SITUATION 🧐

Ibrahim goes to a shop in Kano to buy snacks. He buys a bottle of Fanta for ₦150.50 and a meat pie for ₦350.00. He also buys chewing gum for ₦25.25. To find the total cost, he tries to add the numbers, but gets confused about where to put the decimal point. How can he add them correctly to find the exact total? 🤯

Ibrahim's problem is very common! Adding decimals is easy, but it has one Golden Rule that you must never forget. Once you master this one rule, adding any decimal numbers becomes as simple as adding whole numbers. Today, you will learn this rule and become an expert at calculating totals with decimals! 💪

2. Adding Decimals in Your Daily Life 🛒

Adding decimals is one of the most common maths skills you will use every single day:

Calculating a Shopping Bill

Just like Ibrahim, any time you buy multiple items at a supermarket like Shoprite or a local store, you need to add their prices, which often have decimal parts (Kobo). For example, ₦500.50 + ₦1,200.75. 🛍️

Combining Measurements

If a tailor cuts a piece of fabric that is 2.5 metres long and another that is 1.25 metres long, she adds them together to find the total length of fabric she has used. ✂️

Balancing a Bank Account

When you look at a bank statement, you see deposits and withdrawals. To find your new balance, you add up all the deposits. For example, if you deposit ₦10,000.00 and then another ₦5,500.50, you add the decimals to find your total. 🏦

Tracking Athletic Performance

If an athlete runs 5.25 km in the morning and 3.5 km in the evening, they add these decimals to find the total distance they ran that day. 🏃‍♂️

Science Experiments

In a chemistry lab, a scientist might mix 10.5 ml of one liquid with 5.25 ml of another. Adding these decimals is crucial for the experiment's accuracy. 🔬

3. The Most Important Concept 🔑

  • Lining up the Decimals: This is the GOLDEN RULE of adding (and subtracting) decimals. It means that when you write the numbers down to add them, the decimal points MUST be stacked directly on top of each other in a straight vertical line.
  • Decimal Point: The dot that separates the whole number part (like Naira) from the fractional part (like Kobo). It's the most important landmark in the number!

4. The Golden Rule of Decimal Addition 📜

Let's add two numbers: 12.75 + 3.1.

Step 1: LINE UP THE DECIMAL POINTS! 📍

This is the most important step. Write the numbers one on top of the other, making sure the decimal points are perfectly aligned in a straight column. You can add a zero to the end of 3.1 to make it 3.10 to help with alignment.

  12.75
+  3.10
----

Step 2: Bring the Decimal Point Straight Down. ⬇️

Before you even start adding, bring the decimal point from your question straight down into your answer line. This way, you can't forget it!

  12.75
+  3.10
----
  .

Step 3: Add Normally from Right to Left. ➕

Now, just ignore the decimal point and add the columns exactly like you would with whole numbers, starting from the far right and carrying over when needed.

  12.75
+  3.10
-------
  15.85

The final answer is 15.85.

5. Real-Life Application: Ibrahim's Snack Bill 🛒

Solving the Hook Problem

Let's help Ibrahim calculate his total bill. He bought items for ₦150.50, ₦350.00, and ₦25.25. What is the total cost?

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. Step 1: Line up all three numbers by their decimal points. This is the crucial first step to ensure you are adding Tens to Tens, and Tenths to Tenths. 
      150.50
  350.00
+  25.25
--------
  2. Step 2: Bring the decimal point straight down into the answer line. This reserves its spot.
  3. Step 3: Add the columns from right to left, carrying over where necessary. 
      150.50
  350.00
+  25.25
--------
  525.75

Ibrahim's total bill is ₦525.75.

6. Financial Literacy: Calculating Total Savings 🏦

Summing Up Bank Deposits

A business owner, Mrs. Chidinma, made three deposits into her bank account in one day. The amounts were ₦15,400.50, ₦8,250, and ₦12,000.75. What was the total amount she deposited that day?

Detailed Solution:

Step 1: Write down the numbers, preparing to align them.

Note that ₦8,250 has no decimal part. We can write it as 8250.00 to make lining it up easier.

Step 2: Line up the decimal points perfectly.

  15400.50
   8250.00
+ 12000.75
----------

Step 3: Bring the decimal point down and add the columns.

  15400.50
   8250.00
+ 12000.75
----------
  35651.25

Final Answer: Mrs. Chidinma deposited a total of ₦35,651.25.

7. Pro-Tips & Tricks 💡

The "Pad with Zeros" Technique

When you have numbers with different amounts of decimal places (like 12.5 + 3.456), it can look confusing. The best trick is to "pad" the shorter numbers with zeros at the end until they are all the same length. This doesn't change their value but makes lining them up much easier!

Instead of: 

 12.5
+ 3.456

Write it as: 

 12.500
+  3.456

Now the columns are perfectly aligned and easy to add!

Important Warning!

You can only pad with zeros at the end of a decimal number. Adding zeros in the middle (e.g., changing 3.5 to 3.05) will completely change the value of the number and give you the wrong answer!

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid ❌

The Number One Mistake: Not Lining Up the Decimal Points

This is the most frequent error. If the decimal points are not in a straight vertical line, your entire calculation will be incorrect because you will be adding the wrong place values together (e.g., adding tenths to hundredths).

Wrong Way

A student aligns the numbers to the right, like with whole numbers:

  14.5
+ 2.35

Right Way

The decimal points MUST be aligned, no matter how the numbers look:

 14.50
+ 2.35

9. Practice & Challenge Zone 🧠

Standard Practice Problem

What is 5.8 + 12.15?

Hint: Remember to line up the decimal points. You can pad 5.8 with a zero to make it 5.80 to help with alignment.

   5.80
+ 12.15
-------
  17.95

Answer: 17.95

Challenge Problem 🔥

A tailor bought three pieces of fabric. The first was 4.5 metres, the second was 2.75 metres, and the third was 0.8 metres. What is the total length of all three pieces combined?

Hint: This is a three-part addition. Line up all three numbers by their decimal points. Pad the numbers with zeros so they all have two decimal places (e.g., 4.5 becomes 4.50).

Step 1: Line up the decimals and pad with zeros.

  4.50
  2.75
+ 0.80
------

Step 2: Add from right to left, carrying over as needed.

• Right column: 0 + 5 + 0 = 5.

• Middle column: 5 + 7 + 8 = 20. Write down 0, carry over 2.

• Left column: 2 (carry) + 4 + 2 + 0 = 8.

   2
  4.50
  2.75
+ 0.80
------
  8.05

Final Answer: The total length is 8.05 metres.

10. Summary of Key Steps ✅

To add any decimal numbers correctly:

  1. LINE UP the Decimal Points: This is the Golden Rule! Write the numbers in a column so the decimal points are in a straight vertical line. 📍
  2. Pad with Zeros (Optional but helpful): Add zeros to the end of shorter decimals so all numbers have the same number of decimal places. 👻
  3. Bring Down the Point: Bring the decimal point straight down into your answer line before you start adding. ⬇️
  4. Add Columns: Add each column from right to left, just like you would with whole numbers, carrying over when needed. ➕
