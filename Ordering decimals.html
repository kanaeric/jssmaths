Ordering Decimals

1. The Race to the Finish Line! (Problem to Solve) 🏁

PROBLEM SITUATION 😳

It's the final of the 100-metre race at the school's Inter-House Sports! The times for the top three runners are in:

  • Emeka: 12.1 seconds
  • Bolu: 12.09 seconds
  • Akin: 12.11 seconds

Who came 1st, 2nd, and 3rd? They are so close! How can we put their times in order from fastest to slowest to decide the winner? 🤔

This is where ordering decimals becomes a gold-medal skill! It’s just like comparing, but with more numbers. Let's learn the method to sort out any list of decimals and find the true champion. 🏆

2. Putting Things in Order in Nigeria 🇳🇬

From the market to the classroom, ordering decimals helps make sense of the world.

Ranking Student Scores 🎓

Your teacher needs to rank the top students. If scores are 95.5, 95.8, and 95.45, ordering them from greatest to least tells you who came 1st, 2nd, and 3rd.

Sorting Prices in a Shop 🛍️

You want to find the cheapest bottle of Zobo. By ordering the prices (₦150.50, ₦150.25, ₦155.00) from least to greatest, you can easily spot the best deal.

Listing Ingredients by Weight ⚖️

A complex recipe for Jollof rice might require you to add ingredients in a specific order based on their weight, like 0.25 kg of onions before 0.5 kg of tomatoes.

Analysing Football Team Stats ⚽

To see which Nigerian Professional Football League team is the best, you might order them by their average goals per game: Team A (2.1), Team B (2.05), and Team C (2.15).

Organising Computer Files 💻

When you sort files by size, your computer is ordering decimals! It arranges files like 1.2 MB, 1.15 MB, and 1.21 MB to help you see which is largest.

3. Your Ordering Dictionary 📖

These two terms are the most important instructions in this topic! 🔑

Ascending Order

This means arranging numbers from smallest to largest. Think of a rocket ascending or going up!

Descending Order

This means arranging numbers from largest to smallest. Think of a plane descending or coming down for landing.

Line Up & Pad

This is your master technique! Lining up the decimal points and padding with zeros makes ordering simple and error-free.

4. The Golden Method: Line Up, Pad & Order! 🏆

Let's use our method to put these numbers in ascending order (smallest to largest): 7.8, 7.79, and 7.81.

1

Line Up the Decimal Points

Write all the numbers in a column, making sure the decimal points are perfectly aligned, like buttons on a shirt.

7.8
7.79
7.81
2

Pad with Zeros

Look for the number with the most decimal places (7.79 and 7.81 both have two). Add zeros to the end of all other numbers so they also have two decimal places.

7.80
7.79
7.81

Why? Now we can easily compare the decimal parts as if they were whole numbers: 80, 79, and 81.

3

Compare and Rank

Now, simply find the smallest number, then the next smallest, and so on. The whole number part (7) is the same for all, so we look at the decimal parts.

7.80
7.79     (Smallest)
7.81

Comparing 7.80 and 7.81, we see that 80 is smaller than 81.

4

Write the Final Ordered List

Write the numbers in their original form, from smallest to largest.

The smallest is 7.79.

The next is 7.80, which was originally 7.8.

The largest is 7.81.

Ascending Order: 7.79, 7.8, 7.81

5. Case Study: The Jollof Rice Competition 🍚

Three chefs competed in the Lagos Food Festival's Jollof Rice showdown. The judges' scores are in:

  • Chef Ade: 9.25 points
  • Chef Funke: 9.3 points
  • Chef Emeka: 9.05 points

Can you rank the chefs from 1st place to 3rd place? (This means putting the scores in descending order).

Let's find the winner:

1. Line Up & Pad:

9.25
9.30
9.05

2. Compare and Rank (Largest to Smallest): The whole number (9) is the same for all. Let's look at the decimal parts: 25, 30, and 05.

  • The largest is 30, which corresponds to 9.3.
  • The next largest is 25, which is 9.25.
  • The smallest is 05, which is 9.05.

✅ Final Ranking:

🥇 1st Place: Chef Funke (9.3)

🥈 2nd Place: Chef Ade (9.25)

🥉 3rd Place: Chef Emeka (9.05)

6. Financial Focus: Tracking Airtime Spending 📱

Aisha tracks how much airtime she uses over four days:

  • Monday: ₦150.5
  • Tuesday: ₦150.25
  • Wednesday: ₦155.0
  • Thursday: ₦150.55

Help Aisha list the days from the day she spent the most airtime to the day she spent the least. (Descending order)

Let's sort the spending:

1. Line up the numbers and pad with zeros:

150.50  (Mon)
150.25  (Tue)
155.00  (Wed)
150.55  (Thu)

2. Compare and Rank (Largest to Smallest):

First, look at the whole numbers. 155 is clearly the largest. So Wednesday is the day she spent the most.

Now compare the other three: 150.50, 150.25, and 150.55. Look at the decimal parts: 50, 25, and 55. The largest is 55, then 50, then 25.

✅ Final Order (Most to Least Spent):

  1. Wednesday: ₦155.00
  2. Thursday: ₦150.55
  3. Monday: ₦150.50
  4. Tuesday: ₦150.25

7. Maths Magic: The "Ignore the Dot" Trick ✨

(After You Line Up & Pad!)

Here's a mental shortcut. Once you have correctly lined up the decimals and padded with zeros, you can temporarily ignore the decimal point and just order the numbers you see.

Let's order 12.09, 12.1, and 12.01.

Step 1 (Pad): They become 12.09, 12.10, and 12.01.

Step 2 (The Trick): Now, just think about ordering the numbers 1209, 1210, and 1201. Easy! The order is 1201, 1209, 1210.

So the correct decimal order is 12.01, 12.09, 12.1.

CRITICAL WARNING!

This trick ONLY works AFTER you have padded with zeros to make all decimal parts the same length. If you try to order 12.1 and 12.09 by thinking of 121 and 1209, you will get the wrong answer! The padding step is essential!

8. Watch Out! Ordering Pitfalls! ⚠️

Mistake: Forgetting the Whole Number

Students sometimes get so focused on the decimal part that they forget to check the whole number first. The whole number is ALWAYS more important.

❌ Wrong Way

When ordering 3.9 and 4.1, a student might think "9 is bigger than 1, so 3.9 is bigger."

✅ Right Way

Always check the whole number first! 4 is greater than 3. It doesn't matter what comes after the decimal point. 4.1 is bigger than 3.9.

Mistake: Reading from Right to Left

We read words from left to right, and we must do the same when comparing numbers.

❌ Wrong Way

Comparing 0.25 and 0.31. A student might start from the right and see "5 is bigger than 1," making a wrong conclusion.

✅ Right Way

Start from the left. In the tenths place, 2 is less than 3. Stop there! That's all the information you need. 0.31 is greater than 0.25.

9. Practice & Challenge Zone 🧠

Standard Practice Problem

Arrange these numbers in ascending order (least to greatest):

2.7, 2.73, 2.07

Hint: Line them up and pad 2.7 with a zero to make it 2.70. Now compare 2.70, 2.73, and 2.07. Which is smallest?

1. Line Up & Pad:
2.70
2.73
2.07

2. Compare: The smallest is 2.07 (since 0 in the tenths place is smallest). Between 2.70 and 2.73, 2.70 is smaller.

✅ Final Answer: 2.07, 2.7, 2.73

⭐ Challenge Problem ⭐

Arrange these values from greatest to least (descending order):

0.8, ⅘, 0.85, ⁸/₁₀₀

Hint: First, you must convert all the fractions into decimals. Remember that ⅘ is the same as ⁸/₁₀. Then you can use the Line Up & Pad method on all four numbers.

1. Convert Fractions to Decimals:

  • ⅘ = ⁸/₁₀ = 0.8
  • ⁸/₁₀₀ = 0.08

2. List all decimals: 0.8, 0.8, 0.85, 0.08

3. Line Up & Pad:
0.80
0.80
0.85
0.08

4. Order from Greatest to Least: The largest is 0.85. Next are the two 0.80s. The smallest is 0.08.

✅ Final Answer (using original forms): 0.85, 0.8 (or ⅘), ⁸/₁₀₀

10. Your Golden Rules for Ordering 📜

Remember these three steps and you'll never get a list in the wrong order again!

  1. LINE UP: Write all numbers in a column, with the decimal points perfectly aligned. 📍
  2. PAD: Add zeros to the end of shorter decimals so all have the same number of decimal places. ✍️
  3. COMPARE & RANK: Starting from the left, compare the digits in each column to find the smallest (or largest), then the next, and so on. 🔍
