3. Place Value vs. Value: The Key Difference üîë

It is vital to know the difference between these two related ideas.

  • Place Value: This is the NAME or POSITION of a digit. It's like the "address" of the digit. For decimals, the names have "-ths" at the end: Tenths, Hundredths, Thousandths.
  • Value: This is how much a digit is worth because of its position. It's always a number. If the digit 7 is in the Tenths place, its value is 0.7 or 7/10.

Finding the Place Value and Value of the digit 8

  • Step 1: Locate the digit. Find the digit 8 on our "street map".
  • Step 2: Read its "Address" (Place Value). The 8 is in the second house to the right of the decimal point. Looking at our chart, this is the Hundredths place.
  • Step 3: Determine its "Worth" (Value). The digit is 8 and its place is Hundredths. So its value is "eight hundredths", which we can write as the fraction 8/100 or the decimal 0.08.

7. Pro-Tips & Tricks üí°

The "-ths" Suffix Rule

Here's the easiest way to remember the names of the decimal places: if the place is to the right of the decimal point, its name will almost always end in "-ths".

To the left: Tens, Hundreds, Thousands

To the right: Tenths, Hundredths, Thousandths

That little "th" sound makes all the difference!

Important Warning! The Mirror Image

The decimal places are like a mirror image of the whole number places, but there is NO "oneths" or "uniths" place! The very first place to the right of the decimal point is the Tenths place.

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid ‚ùå

Mistake 1: Confusing "Tens" and "Tenths"

This is the most common mistake and it comes from misreading. The "ths" is critical.

Wrong Way

In 20.2, a student might say the place value of both 2s is "Tens".

Right Way

The '2' on the left is in the Tens place. The '2' on the right is in the Tenths place.

Mistake 2: Confusing Place Value and Value

Remember, one is a name (an address) and the other is a number (how much it's worth).

Wrong Way

Question: What is the place value of the 3 in 0.3?
Wrong Answer: 0.3.

Right Way

Question: What is the place value of the 3 in 0.3?
Correct Answer: Tenths.

Step 2: Find the value of the second 5.

In 1.05, the 5 is in the Hundredths place. Its value is 0.05.

Step 3: Calculate how many times larger.

We need to divide the larger value by the smaller value: 0.5 √∑ 0.05.

This is the same as asking, "How many 5 Kobo coins are in 50 Kobo?" The answer is 10.

Final Answer: The value of the 5 in 2.5 is 10 times greater than the value of the 5 in 1.05.
