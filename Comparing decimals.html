Comparing Decimals

1. The Savings Showdown! (Problem to Solve) 💰

PROBLEM SITUATION 😳

Tunde and Sade are saving money in their piggy banks. Tunde proudly opens his and counts ₦150.75. Sade counts hers and finds she has ₦150.8. Tunde thinks he has more because 75 is a bigger number than 8. Is he correct? Who actually has more money? 🤔

This is a very common mix-up! Comparing numbers with decimal points isn't always what it seems. Today, you will learn the foolproof method to compare any two decimal numbers and always find the correct answer. Let the showdown begin! 🚀

2. Comparing Decimals in Nigeria 🇳🇬

Knowing which decimal is bigger is a super important skill you use all the time!

Shopping for the Best Price 🛒

One pack of Indomie is ₦120.50 at Shop A, and ₦120.75 at Shop B. Comparing decimals helps you know that Shop A has the cheaper price.

Athletics Race Results 🏃‍♂️

In a race, Emeka finishes in 11.3 seconds and Bolu finishes in 11.25 seconds. By comparing, you know Bolu was faster because his time is the smaller number.

Baking Ingredients 🍰

A recipe needs 0.5 kg of sugar, but you only have 0.45 kg. Comparing these tells you that you don't have enough sugar for the recipe.

Comparing Phone Specs 📱

One phone has a 2.4 GHz processor and another has a 2.35 GHz processor. The first phone is slightly faster because 2.4 is greater than 2.35.

Checking Fuel Efficiency 🚗

Car A travels 12.5 km per litre, while Car B travels 12.45 km per litre. Car A is more fuel-efficient because 12.5 is greater than 12.45.

3. Your Comparison Dictionary 📖

Let's get our key terms and symbols right before we start comparing. 🔑

Greater Than (>)

This symbol means the number on the left is bigger than the number on the right. Think of it as an alligator's mouth wanting to eat the bigger number! Example: 5 > 3.

Less Than (<)

This symbol means the number on the left is smaller than the number on the right. The alligator is facing the other way! Example: 3 < 5.

Equal To (=)

This means both numbers have the exact same value, even if they look a little different. Example: 0.5 = 0.50.

Place Value

The value of a digit based on its position. When comparing, we check the digits in the same place value against each other (Ones vs Ones, Tenths vs Tenths, etc.).

4. The Golden Method: Line Up & Compare! 🏆

Let's solve Tunde's and Sade's problem from the start: Which is greater, 150.75 or 150.8? This method works every single time!

1

Line Up the Decimal Points

Write the numbers one on top of the other, making sure the decimal points are perfectly aligned. This is the most important step!

150.75
150.8
2

Pad with Zeros (If Needed)

Add zeros to the end of any shorter decimal so that both numbers have the same number of decimal places. This makes them easier to compare visually.

150.75
150.80

Why? Adding a zero to the end of a decimal doesn't change its value (just like ₦150.8 is the same as ₦150.80). But it helps avoid the mistake of comparing 75 to 8!

3

Compare from Left to Right

Start from the leftmost digit and compare the numbers in each place value column, one by one, until you find a difference.

150.75
150.80
  • Hundreds place: 1 vs 1 (Same)
  • Tens place: 5 vs 5 (Same)
  • Ones place: 0 vs 0 (Same)
  • Tenths place: 7 vs 8 (Different!)

We found a difference! 8 is greater than 7. We can stop here. The second number is bigger.

So, 150.80 is greater than 150.75. Sade has more money!

5. Case Study: The 100-Metre Dash 🏃‍♀️

At the All-Nigeria Athletics Championship, two star athletes, Favour and Grace, are competing in the 100m final.

  • Favour's time: 11.09 seconds
  • Grace's time: 11.1 seconds

Who won the race? (Remember: in a race, the smaller time is the faster time!)

Let's compare their times:

Step 1: Line Up the Dots.

11.09
11.1

Step 2: Pad with Zeros.

11.09
11.10

Step 3: Compare Left to Right.

11.09
11.10

The Tens and Ones places are the same. In the Tenths place, 0 is less than 1. This means 11.09 is the smaller number.

✅ Final Answer: Favour won the race with the faster time of 11.09 seconds!

6. Financial Focus: Finding the Best Deal 🛒

Mr. Eze wants to buy a 5kg bag of rice. He checks two supermarkets:

  • Shop A sells it for ₦8550.5
  • Shop B sells it for ₦8550.25

Which shop offers the better, cheaper price for the rice?

Let's find the cheaper price:

1. Line up the numbers and pad with a zero:

8550.50
8550.25

2. Compare from left to right: The whole number parts (8550) are the same.

3. Compare the Tenths place:

8550.50
8550.25

We see that 2 is less than 5. This means 8550.25 is the smaller, cheaper price.

✅ Final Answer: Shop B has the better deal. Mr. Eze should buy his rice there!

7. Maths Magic: The "Add Zeros" Trick! ✨

Make Them Look Alike!

The fastest way to avoid mistakes is to make the decimal parts the same length by adding zeros.

Let's compare 0.9 and 0.89.

The decimal part of 0.9 has one digit. The decimal part of 0.89 has two digits.

Add a zero to 0.9 to make it 0.90.

Now, which is bigger: 90 or 89? It's easy! 90 is bigger. So, 0.9 is greater than 0.89.

Quick Warning!

This trick only works after the decimal point! You can add zeros to the end of a decimal (0.9 = 0.90 = 0.900) but you cannot add zeros at the beginning (0.9 is NOT the same as 0.09)!

8. Watch Out! The "Longer is Bigger" Trap! ⚠️

Mistake: Thinking More Digits Means a Bigger Number

This is the most common trap in comparing decimals. Let's go back to Tunde's problem: 150.75 vs 150.8.

❌ Wrong Way

Ignoring the decimal point and thinking "75 is bigger than 8, so 150.75 must be bigger." This is incorrect!

✅ Right Way

Line them up:
150.75
150.80

Now compare the tenths place: 7 vs 8. 8 is bigger. Therefore, 150.8 is greater than 150.75.

9. Practice & Challenge Zone 🧠

Standard Practice Problem

Which symbol (<, >, or =) should go in the blank?
4.5 ___ 4.49

Hint: Use the "Add Zeros" trick. Change 4.5 to 4.50. Now, which is bigger, 4.50 or 4.49?

1. Line Up & Pad:
4.50
4.49

2. Compare: The Ones place is the same (4). In the Tenths place, 5 is greater than 4.

✅ Final Answer: 4.5 > 4.49

⭐ Challenge Problem ⭐

Arrange these lengths from least to greatest:

1.05 m, 1.5 m, 1.055 m, 1.505 m

Hint: Line all four numbers up by their decimal points. Pad all of them with zeros until they all have three decimal places (e.g., 1.5 becomes 1.500). Then compare them row by row.

1. Line Up & Pad with Zeros:

1.050
1.500
1.055
1.505

2. Compare and Order:

  • The smallest numbers are the ones with 0 in the tenths place. Between 1.050 and 1.055, 1.050 is smaller.
  • Next is 1.055.
  • Now look at the numbers with 5 in the tenths place. Between 1.500 and 1.505, 1.500 (or 1.5) is smaller.
  • The largest is 1.505.

✅ Final Answer (Least to Greatest): 1.05 m, 1.055 m, 1.5 m, 1.505 m

10. Your Golden Rules for Comparing Decimals 📜

Always remember these three foolproof steps!

  1. LINE UP: Write the numbers vertically, lining up the decimal points perfectly. 📍
  2. PAD: Add zeros to the end of shorter decimals so all numbers have the same length after the point. ✍️
  3. COMPARE: Start from the left and compare the digits in each place value column until you find a difference. The first difference decides which is bigger! 🔍
